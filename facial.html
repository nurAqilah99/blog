1. If You’re Acne-Prone, Sop Up Oil With a Clay Mask

When you battle breakouts on the regular, you’re always looking for the next great thing to help clear your skin. In reality, a mask is only going to do so much. “It’s important to find a daily regimen to keep acne under control, [rather than rely solely] on a weekly mask,” says Dr. Waldorf.

Still, using a clay-based mask — she likes Caudalie’s Purifying Mask — “can help make skin look and feel better,” she says. Waldorf points to its ingredients like sage, which helps fight inflammation, according to past research; zinc, which a review published in July 2014 in Dermatology

Research and Practice showed can fight bacteria; and of course clay, which an earlier study showed can help purge impurities from pores by making you sweat. These ingredients work together to banish breakouts at the source.



2. If Your Skin Needs a Boost of Brightness, Go for a Gentle Exfoliant

When dead cells on your skin’s outer layer build up, you may notice that your complexion looks dull. If you have pigmentation problems or acne that includes blackheads or whiteheads, you may additionally benefit from exfoliation, which sloughs off these old cells and reveals newer, brighterlooking cells underneath, according to the American Academy of Dermatology (AAD). “I typically like masks that balance moisturization alongside an active ingredient that’s a mild exfoliant or an alpha hydroxy acid (AHA),” says Dr. Hollmig.

If you’re not familiar, AHAs are a class of chemical compounds that includes glycolic and lactic acid, per CosmeticsInfo.org. (These differ from beta hydroxy acids, or BHAs, the most famous of which is anti-acne salicylic acid.) Because some people find that the process of exfoliation can be irritating, meaning it causes your skin to burn or become red, you can counter that potential reaction with a good moisturizer.

Glow Recipe Watermelon and AHA Glow Sleeping Mask is one example. It supplies AHAs (thanks to lactic and glycolic acids, a study noted) to remove dead cells and even tone, plus hyaluronic acid for moisturizing, as a study published in May 2016 in the German journal MMW Advances Med found. Another tip to counteract possible irritation, says Hollmig, is to use an AHA mask weekly or a couple of times per week before using it daily.



3. To Address Hyperpigmentation, Look for Specific Botanicals

Discoloration can be from a pimple that left a red mark behind or the result of too much sun exposure that led to dark spots, according to the AAD. Treating your skin means interrupting the pigmentation process, from stopping cells from pumping out melanin (the pigment in your skin)

to breaking it down at its surface. Along with adequate sun protection (to stop darkness from worsening), there are several plant-based ingredients that work as lighteners. Mario Badescu’s Whitening Mask contains kojic acid, mulberry, and licorice root. Research published in the Journal of Cutaneous and Aesthetic Surgery showed that all three of these ingredients show promise in attacking pigment by inhibiting melanin production, reducing inflammation, and providing antioxidant properties.



4. If You Have Sensitive Skin, Try One With Skin Soothers

When your skin reacts easily and erupts in redness, stinging, burning, or itching in response to environmental factors (from heat to sun to topical products), it may be sensitive, noted a study published in March 2016 in Acta Dermato-Venerologica. As such, it can be tough to find something compatible with your complexion.

Waldorf recommends going for a mask that’s moisturizing only. It’s also helpful to look for one that’s hypoallergenic, which means it’s less likely to trigger irritation. Avène Soothing Sheet Mask is alcohol- and fragrance-free, has a short list of ingredients (so there’s less of a chance something is in there to cause a reaction), and it calms with thermal spring water. In a past review, researchers noted that this water, which naturally contains trace minerals, acts as an anti-inflammatory and reduces the immune response that triggers symptoms.



5. If You Have Dry Skin, Look for Ingredients Like Hyaluronic Acid

The good news if you have skin prone to dryness is that it’s perfectly suited for a mask. “Face masks represent an amazing tool for augmenting the natural skin barrier and retaining moisture,” says Hollmig. Dry skin is more than simply uncomfortable. As the AAD points out, dry skin can crack, allowing irritants and germs into skin that can even lead to an infection. Masks are occlusive — something that sits on top of skin and

prevents moisture loss. After using one, you’ll likely see that your skin “becomes less dry and flaky, and more plump and moist,” he says. Hollmig recommends looking for a mask that contains top-notch hydrators like hyaluronic acid, ceramides, or squalene. For example, ceramides have been shown in research to help repair skin’s barrier, noted a study published in February 2018 in Pharmaceutical Research.

Yet watch for ingredients like AHAs, which as an exfoliator can cause peeling, so you want to observe how your skin reacts when using them, says Hollmig. One option is the immensely popular My Skin Mentor Dr. G Aqua Collagen Mask, sold by the Korean beauty website Soko Glam. The sheet mask hydrates with hyaluronic acid, glycerin, and aloe leaf extract.



6.If You’re Looking to Reduce Fine Lines and Wrinkles, Look for Silicone

Fine lines and wrinkles appear after collagen and elastin, the supportive structures within skin, begin to break down, according to Dermstore.com. In addition, skin becomes drier, something that exacerbates the look of lines. That said, a mask can only do so much when it comes to wrinkles. Most of the benefit from masks comes from the fact that they address the appearance of lines by hydrating skin, says Waldorf. One sound option,

says Hollmig, is a silicone pad, which plumps up the outer layer of skin to help smooth out wrinkles, he explains. (Wrinkles Schminkles Forehead Wrinkle Remover Pads are one such product.) “Silicone pads absolutely help smooth forehead lines,” Hollmig says.

Fair warning: “I would expect results to be only temporary, just like a topical moisturizer,” he says. These do not contain an active ingredient to stimulate collagen production, so you’ll see a short-term benefit from the hydration. The benefit is that you can use these in targeted ways, like before a night out or important event.



7. If Your Skin Is Dull and Parched From Traveling, Look for Antioxidants

One of the big benefits of a mask is “their ability to naturally moisturize the skin by helping the outer layer reduce 'transepidermal water loss,' which is the loss of natural moisture when water evaporates from skin,” says Hollmig. That’s likely to happen if you’re traveling on an airplane. “Traveling is really hard on the skin. Airplane air is super dry, which make the skin dull and can even cause cracking and irritation,” he says.

(Forgetting to hydrate by drinking water can also contribute to dullness.) And, just like the whole travel process can cause psychological stress and zap your sleep, it also physically stresses skin by forcing it to adapt to a new environment, Hollmig adds.

In addition to moisturizing, you’ll want to brighten jet-lagged skin with antioxidants such as vitamin C. Summer Fridays Jet Lag Mask combines antioxidant vitamin C to renew radiance and sodium hyaluronate (a form of hyaluronic acid) to restore moisture. It’s also gentle enough to be applied on the eye area and doesn’t need to be washed off (just rub in excess), which is helpful when you’re mid-journey.
